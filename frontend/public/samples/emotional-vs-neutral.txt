The letter arrived on a Tuesday morning, and when she opened it, her hands began to tremble. It was from her estranged father, the one who had vanished when she was seven years old. Twenty-three years of silence, of birthdays without cards, of watching other children run to their fathers at school pickup. The words blurred through her tears: "I am so deeply sorry. Not a single day has passed where I didn't think of you. I was afraid, broken, and selfish. You deserved so much more than what I gave you, which was nothing." She pressed the paper against her chest and sobbed, grief and relief and rage all tangled together in a sound she didn't recognize as her own.
