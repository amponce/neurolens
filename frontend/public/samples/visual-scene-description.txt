The ancient temple emerged from the jungle canopy like a stone giant rising from a green sea. Massive columns carved with serpentine dragons supported a roof thick with hanging moss and orchids in violent shades of purple and crimson. Shafts of golden afternoon light pierced through gaps in the canopy above, casting long diagonal beams through the mist that hung in the air like gauze curtains. The floor was a mosaic of turquoise and obsidian tiles, many cracked and pushed apart by centuries of root growth, with ferns unfurling from every crevice.

At the center of the chamber stood a pool of perfectly still water, its surface a flawless mirror reflecting the vaulted ceiling above. A single lotus flower, impossibly white against the dark water, floated near the far edge. The walls were alive with bas-relief carvings depicting celestial scenes: galaxies rendered as spiraling mandalas, constellations connected by flowing lines, and a enormous sun with rays that seemed to ripple and move as your eyes traveled across the stone. Tiny blue butterflies drifted through the space in lazy spirals, their wings catching the light and flashing like fragments of sapphire scattered on an invisible wind.

Beyond the temple's open eastern wall, the landscape dropped away into a vast valley filled with terraced rice paddies that descended in geometric steps, each one a slightly different shade of green, from the bright chartreuse of new growth to the deep emerald of mature plants. A river wound through the valley floor, silver and sinuous, and in the far distance, volcanic peaks rose into clouds that glowed pink and amber in the fading light.
